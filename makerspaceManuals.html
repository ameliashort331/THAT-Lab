Makerspace Manuals


These resources were created by Ruipu Hu and Alisha Pradhan through a co-design process with older adults in a retirement community. The manuals were created to support activities offered at the resident-run makerspace and are currently in use. We intentionally selected activities which range from technologically complex (Cricut cutting machine) to non-technological activities such as button making.

Cricut (cutting machine): part 1 and part 2
Sewing machine: Standard operating procedure
Button maker: Standard operating procedure
Jewelry making: Earrings and Necklaces

Acknowledgements: This project was funded in part by grant 90REGE0008, U.S. Admin. for Community Living, NIDILRR, Dept. of Health & Human Services as well as NSF grant IIS-1816145.


The Health, Aging, and Technology Lab is located in the College of Information Studies at the University of Maryland. We are part of the Human Computer Interaction Lab and Trace Research & Development Center.
